<p style="font-size:1.1em; text-align:justify;"> I am a postdoctoral researcher at École Normale Supérieure (ENS), Paris, France, where I develop and apply mathematical models to understand the dynamics of <b>biogeochemical cycles</b> in terrestrial ecosystems.
</p>

<p style="font-size:1.1em; text-align:justify;">
My research focuses on how climate variability and land management practices influence soil carbon and nutrient dynamics, with the overarching goal of improving predictions of <b>ecosystem responses under global change</b>.
</p>
<p style="font-size:1.1em; text-align:justify;">
I am particularly interested in mechanisms regulating soil organic matter turnover, the interactions between <b>microbial processes and environmental drivers</b>, and how these insights can inform sustainable land management and climate mitigation strategies.
</p>
<p style="font-size:1.1em; text-align:justify;">
I am passionate about linking <b>theory and data</b>, and about communicating complex systems science in accessible ways through talks, collaborations, and teaching.
</p>
